论文笔记系列之九：Classification of Schizophrenia Versus Normal Subjects Using Deep Learning（Proceedings of the Tenth Indian Conference on Computer Vision，2016）

Posted on 2017-08-29 | In papers |

Abstract

tips:

  • fMRI stacked autoencoder(SAD)
  • Basic Method:A separate 4-hidden layer autoencoder is trained in an unsupervised manner for feature extraction corresponding to every brain region. Then, SVM classifier
  • In order to design a robust classifier, used covariance based approach
  • Dataset: 1000
  • Method: 10 fold CV
  • Result: accuracy:92%, better than existing methods

1. Introduction

There is no standard automated fMRI tool in hospitals that can be used for disease diagnosis.
Cobre dataset Schizophrenia and normal subjects

In 12 , DNN with mutiple hidden layers along with sparsity constraint is used to classify subjects.
limitition: Mean time series (all voxels in a whole region may not be active at any time), Pearson Correlation (neglect the effect of other regions).

In 1, ICA +various graph theoretic measures. 65% accuracy

In this paper,

  1. propose covariance based approach to filter out inactive GM form the fMRI dataset before the data is fed to the first stage of the classifier
  2. 2-stage classifier: first stage, autoencoder(4 hidden layers) trained on every brain region(116 regions), output is 8-length feature vectors for each region. Second stage, SVM classifier.
  3. Instead of using the mean time series of all voxels, we have used complete time series of all active voxels as input to SAEs.

3. Data description and pre-processing

COBRE dataset
74 HC + 72 SZ
voxel size : 334

proprocess: SPM8 , discard first 5 volumes ….

Further, smooth: FWHM=8mm

4. Proposed work

Pre-state: filter out inactive or noisy GM voxels
Stage1: auto encoder for every region
Stage2: SVM

4.1 Pre-stage

$$
K_y[l]= \dfrac{E[(y[n]-\mu[n])(y[n-l]-\mu[n-l])]}{\sigma_y^2}
$$

Compute the area under the curve wxhibited by $ K_y[l] $ , $l= [-35,35] $. If the area over 2, the voxel is noisy.
